The Research Question

Traditional SEO meets LLM-powered search

Traditional SEO

Search engines rank pages using crawl-based signals: backlinks, keyword density, page speed, freshness, domain authority.

  • Optimized for crawlers and ranking algorithms
  • Rewards link-building, technical SEO, freshness
  • Decades of established best practices

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)

LLMs re-rank search results by reading titles and snippets. They evaluate content quality from what they see, not from link graphs.

  • LLMs see only: position, domain, title, snippet
  • Different signals may matter (structure, relevance, clarity)
  • No one systematic approach

Core question: When an LLM re-ranks search results, which on-page features causally determine whether a page gets promoted or demoted?

Methodology: Double Machine Learning

Causal inference, not just correlation

1
Predict outcome (Y) from confounders (X)
Take residuals: variation in rankings unexplained by confounders
2
Predict treatment (D) from confounders (X)
Take residuals: variation in treatment not driven by confounders
3
Regress residual-Y on residual-D
The slope = causal effect, net of all confounding

Partially Linear Regression (PLR) with 5-fold cross-fitting.
Nuisance functions estimated by LightGBM and Random Forest.

Key Insight: Cross-Reference Reveals Origin

By testing the same treatment on three outcomes (pre_rank, post_rank, rank_delta), we see where the signal comes from:

  • T2 Headings: Neither SERP nor LLM significant alone, but the gap is
  • T3 Schema: Pure LLM effect (SERP indifferent, p=0.80)
  • T1 Stats: LLM-specific penalty (small but consistent)

These results shaped the expansion: Do they replicate with more data, different LLMs, different engines, and different pool sizes?

The Counterintuitive Findings

What SEO “best practices” actually hurt in LLM re-ranking

Surprise #1: Freshness Hurts

Traditional SEO rewards fresh content. But LLMs demote recently updated pages by ~0.056 positions per freshness level. Significant in 8 of 10 analysis subsets.

Surprise #2: Schema Markup

Code-detected structured data (JSON-LD) promotes by +0.15 positions. But the expanded LLM-read measure of the same thing demotes by −0.12.

Surprise #3: Citations Don't Help

Outbound links to .edu/.gov/academic domains show a consistent negative effect. The LLM can't follow links

The Winner: Topic Match > Everything

Topical competence (semantic similarity between page text and query) gains +0.61 positions. Consistent direction across runs. Qwen responds even stronger (+0.73).

Robustness Checks

Results hold across engines, models, pool sizes, and ML learners

Engine Invariance

DuckDuckGo and SearXNG produce the same directional effects. The LLM re-ranker dominates

Llama-3.3-70B and Qwen2.5-72B agree on all major effects. Both are 70B-class instruction-tuned models

Model Fit: Low R² Is Expected

OLS R² = 3–7%. Most ranking variance comes from unmeasured factors (exact relevance, backlinks, brand). Low R² does not invalidate significant effectssmall but consistent signal in a noisy world. We need the measures from SEO API to get a fuller picture of the causal landscape.

Actionable Recommendations for GEO

What to tell your content team based on causal evidence

DO: High-Impact Actions

  • Write for topical depth, not keywords. Topical competence (+0.61 positions) is the strongest positive signal. Cover the query comprehensively.
  • Be the primary source. Earned media (reviews, press, aggregators) is demoted −1.68 positions. First-party authoritative content wins.
  • Use clean JSON-LD schema. Well-formed FAQ/Product/HowTo markup helps (+0.15). Keep it simple and accurate.
  • Structure with question headings. H2/H3 tags phrased as natural-language questions gain +0.10 positions.
  • Favor evergreen depth over recency. Don't aggressively date-stamp content. LLMs prefer authority over freshness.

DON'T: Common Mistakes

  • Don't rely on third-party coverage. Earned media (G2, Capterra, Forbes, Reddit) loses −1.68 positions. The LLM wants the official source, not a review.
  • Don't over-optimize freshness signals. Aggressive “last updated” manipulation actually hurts (−0.056 per level).
  • Don't stuff structured data. Excessive/complex schemas backfire (−0.14). Quality over quantity.
  • Don't expect citation links to help. Outbound links to .edu/.gov don't improve LLM rankings (−0.019)
  • Don't pack pages with numbers. Dense statistics have a small negative effect (−0.017). Focus on clarity.

The Bottom Line

LLMs reward substance over signals and primary sources over third-party coverage. The biggest wins: topical depth (+0.61) and being the official source (earned media loses −1.68). Traditional SEO signals (freshness, external citations, keyword density) either don't help or actively hurt.

Expansion

Next-phase data enrichment to sharpen causal estimates

SEMRush API

Page-level and domain-level signals we currently lack.

  • Backlink authority score per page (replaces our proxy domain authority)
  • Referring domains count (external link graph depth)
  • Page-level organic traffic (real visibility, not just rank)
  • Query intent classification (informational / navigational / transactional / commercial)
  • Domain Authority & Trust Score at the domain level
  • Keyword difficulty & search volume per query

LLM Feature Extraction (2nd pass)

Re-read cached HTML with targeted prompts to extract content-quality signals.

  • Inverted pyramid / conclusion-first score (does the page lead with the answer?)
  • Promotional tone index (neutral editorial vs. sales-heavy copy)
  • Earned media classification (LLM-based, replacing the hardcoded 249-domain list)
  • Content depth score (surface-level listicle vs. comprehensive guide)
  • Jargon density (accessibility vs. technical depth trade-off)

Why backlinks matter

We currently lack inbound link data entirely. SEMRush gives us backlink signals, letting us test whether the LLM implicitly correlates with backlink authority or ignores it entirely.

Why LLM-based earned media

The hardcoded domain list (T7) misses niche review blogs and false-positives on brands with editorial arms. An LLM reading the actual page can classify source type from content, not domain name.

Why promotional tone

We can define earned media as a tiem of content that is derivated and/or aggregated of content from other pages and domains. We also want to measure how promotional content is handled.

Data infrastructure: cached HTML already available for LLM re-extraction. SEMRush API requires commercial subscription (batch endpoint for ~15K domains).
