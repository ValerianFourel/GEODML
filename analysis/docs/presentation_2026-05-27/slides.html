Causal DML for LLM-driven Search Re-ranking

Which on-page content features causally change whether an LLM keeps a URL — and how far it moves it — in a SERP rerank?

Double / Debiased ML Llama-3.3-70B · Qwen-2.5-72B DuckDuckGo · SearXNG 1,011 keywords · 4 variants

Valerian Fourel · Hamburg

What we estimate, and against what.

  • T — six content treatments (stats, Q-headings, schema, citations, topical comp., freshness)
  • X — 28 confounders (BM25, DA, backlinks, intent, …) — block the back-door
  • Y₂ = rankpost — LLM-assigned position

Estimand: the average effect of each content treatment on the LLM's rerank decision, holding the SERP baseline and confounders fixed.

Setup · framework

From SERP query to causal coefficients — one diagram.

  • Inputs: SERP from DDG / SearXNG → rankpre
  • 6 content treatments T · 28 confounders X
  • LLM reranker: Llama-3.3-70B + Qwen-2.5-72B, prompt ∈ {biased, neutral}, evidence ∈ {snippet, RAG}
  • Three outcomes: Y₁ admission, Y₂ Δrank, Y₃ rankpost
  • θ̂T = DML(T, Y; X) · cross-fitted LightGBM, K=5, robust SEs via influence function
  • marginal = single T · mutually-controlled = joint with the other 5 T
Why DML: orthogonalises both the treatment-on-X and outcome-on-X nuisance fits — coefficients are valid under flexible non-linear confounding.

Why mutual control matters
Methodology · marginal vs partial

Single-treatment DML (◯) vs mutually-controlled DML (▢) for all three outcomes.

  • T₅ topical: marginal small → partial large positive on Y₁/Y₃ — masked by other T
  • T₂ Q-headings: stable across both fits — robust signal
  • T₃ schema: marginal more extreme — multicollinearity inflated it
  • Multiple sign-stable cells move >50% on magnitude
Mutual control is not cosmetic: single-T DML systematically over- or under-states effects when treatments correlate. The paper's headline uses the partial fit.

Saliency — does the LLM attend to what it acts on?
Mechanism · attention

Saliency ratio = attention on treatment tokens / attention on other tokens.

  • T₁ stats density (null) — Qwen 1.93×  Llama 0.89×
  • T₂ Q-headings (promoter) — ~1× both models
  • T₃ schema (demoter) — Llama 0.19×  Qwen 0.40×
  • Attention ≠ effect direction: demoters get ignored, but null tokens can be attended-to
Attention is not effect: Q-headings change the decision without dominating attention; schema gets ignored — yet causally demotes. Two different channels.

The predictive-method R² — one feature carries the outcome
Diagnostics · LightGBM nuisance R²
Leave-one-out ΔR² on Y₂ Δrank
0.655
conf_serp_position alone explains 65.5% of Δrank variance under the cross-fitted LightGBM nuisance fit.
0.781full R² with all 28 confounders 0.126R² when conf_serp_position is dropped 0.025combined ΔR² of the other 27 confounders
i.e. 84% of Y₂'s explainable variance is one feature; the remaining 27 confounders contribute the last 3 pp.

The cross-fitted LightGBM that powers the nuisance fits is, on Δrank, almost a one-feature model.

  • Δrank is anchored. The LLM moves URLs much less than they were originally ranked, so a regressor that just knows rankpre (≈ conf_serp_position) already nails most of the variance.
  • Why this matters. A naive OLS on Δrank would attribute that 0.655 of anchoring variance to whichever treatment is co-correlated. DML orthogonalises it away first.
  • Sanity check on the other outcomes. Y₃ rankpost R² = 0.36, Y₁ admission R² = 0.33 — far less anchored, more LLM agency, smaller risk of confounder hijack.
  • Predictive ≠ causal. A 0.78-R² model on Δrank still says nothing about which content features caused the move; that's what the residual stage of DML is for.
The cleanest answer to "why DML, not OLS?" — because on Δrank, one anchoring feature would dominate any naive regression. DML strips it out before the treatments are even allowed to compete.

Four robust findings

Mutually-controlled DML + joint inference + two LLMs + two engines.

  • T₂ Q-headings  causally promote admission and rank (+)
  • T₅ topical comp.  kept-but-downranked — mixed but always significant
  • T₃ schema (JSON-LD)  demotes admission, hurts final position
  • T₆ freshness  demotes admission, especially under biased prompts
